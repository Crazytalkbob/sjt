SpaceJunk Technologies

Making the world a better place through space junk disposal.

What is this?

Ever since the first missions to space, humanity has been leaving trails of debris circling the Earth.

This debris is dangerous to future space missions and to our satellites and space stations that are currently in orbit.

Our mission is to clean up space debris by blowing it ou of the sky.

We get our funding through government grants and donations from wonderful individuals like you!

Find out more about who we are and why we do what we do below.

Why space junk?

The speed of sound on Earth is approximately 768 mph.

The fragments in space travel at least 20 times that speed at speeds up to 18,000 mph.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How do you plan on removing space debris?
  • We plan on launching rockets into space that will blow up when they hit the space debris.
  • Isn't that kind of dangerous?
  • It certainly is, but it's also really cool!
  • Won't blowing up debris create thousands of pieces of additional debris?
  • Yes! But our calculations tell us that 40% of that debris will fall to Earth, while burning up in the process.
  • But that leaves 60% of the pieces. Aren't those pieces just as dangerous to astronauts and equipment?
  • Absolutely! And we look forward to sending more rockets to blow up those pieces in the future.
  • Is this for real?
  • No, none of this is real (except for the actual space debris floating above our heads).
